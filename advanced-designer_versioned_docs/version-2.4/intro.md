# Setics STTAR Advanced Designer

**Advanced Designer** (license code `STAD`) is the interactive desktop
workspace for designing and optimizing fiber-optic access networks
(FTTH/FTTx). It combines a geographic map, a structured network model, and an
automated design engine so you can take a project from raw input data to a
costed, buildable network design and its engineering deliverables.

## What you can do with Advanced Designer

- **Bring in your geography** — load background maps (OpenStreetMap or your own
  tile server) and import the input data for an area: pathways, nodes,
  endpoints (premises) and terminations.
- **Model the physical infrastructure** — pathways, ducts and conduits, and
  access structures (joints, poles, cabinets), including obstacles and cost
  penalties.
- **Design the active network** — place and dimension network nodes and
  endpoints, build the splitter/coupler hierarchy, and define service areas.
- **Let the engine do the heavy lifting** — automatic routing, dimensioning and
  cabling, driven by your design rules and the project equipment database.
- **Check and validate** — run the validation engine against your design
  instructions and resolve the issues it flags.
- **Produce deliverables** — engineering reports (cables, devices, splice
  diagrams, service areas), business reports (cost breakdown, project summary),
  and interoperability exports (GIS/shapefile, FME).

## The STTAR product family

Advanced Designer is the full interactive application. Two siblings share the
same model and engine:

| Product | License | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Advanced Designer** | `STAD` | Full interactive desktop design & optimization. |
| **Planner** | `STPL` | A restricted operator profile of the same app (editing and optimization capabilities are limited). |
| **Batch Engine** | `STBE` | Command-line / batch processing for automated runs. |

> Your license unlocks the manual for the product you own. Use the **version
> picker** in the navbar to switch between documented STTAR releases — each
> version has its own **What changed** page.
